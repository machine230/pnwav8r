How It Works

Five capabilities. One enforcement layer.

Clearance sits between every agent your organization deploys and everything those agents touch. Every capability below is active for every registered agent, from day one.

01
Agent Registry

Every agent your organization runs is registered, assigned an owner, versioned, and tracked. You know exactly what you have deployed, who authorized it, and when it was last updated. Unregistered agents cannot call through the proxy.

INVENTORY
02
Policy Proxy

Every outbound call an agent makes passes through Clearance before execution. Calls are checked against your policy. Allowed calls proceed. Denied calls are blocked and logged with full context. Nothing bypasses the proxy.

ENFORCEMENT
03
Tamper-Evident Audit Log

Every decision Clearance makes is signed and hash-chained. The log cannot be altered after the fact. Export it for an auditor, a regulator, or an incident investigation. What happened is exactly what the log says happened.

AUDIT TRAIL
04
Drift Detection and Suspend

When an agent begins behaving outside its authorized parameters, Clearance suspends it automatically. No human has to catch it in the act. The agent stays suspended until a human explicitly reviews the situation and resumes it. No agent resumes itself.

Authorized parameters are defined at registration and versioned alongside the agent. Drift is measured against the registered baseline, not a generic anomaly model.

AUTO-SUSPEND
05
Compliance Intelligence

Agent activity is mapped to specific control requirements: NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act Articles 12 and 14, FINRA 2026, and PCI DSS. Clearance generates evidence packages for each framework. Audit preparation becomes a report, not a project.

EVIDENCE

Regulatory Readiness

Built to answer the
requirements directly.

The regulations below are active or imminent. Each one names specific obligations around AI agent logging, traceability, and human oversight. Clearance is built against these requirements, not around them.

FINRA 2026 AI Traceability

FINRA's 2026 guidance requires firms to maintain records of AI system actions, demonstrate traceability of decisions, and show that human oversight mechanisms are in place for any AI system operating in a regulated capacity. Clearance's audit log and suspend mechanism are built to satisfy these requirements directly.

EU AI Act Art. 12 Logging Requirements

Article 12 requires high-risk AI systems to maintain automatic logs sufficient to assess compliance with the Act throughout the system's lifetime. Clearance generates those logs automatically. Every entry is signed and hash-chained so the record is credible to a regulator who needs to verify it was not modified after the fact.

EU AI Act Art. 14 Human Oversight

Article 14 requires that high-risk AI systems be designed to allow natural persons to oversee them and intervene when necessary. Clearance's drift detection, auto-suspend, and human-resume mechanism is purpose-built to satisfy this requirement. An agent that drifts is stopped. A human decides when it can proceed.

NIST AI RMF AI Risk Management

The NIST AI Risk Management Framework defines four functions: GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE. Clearance's registry, policy proxy, audit log, and compliance intelligence layer produce evidence across all four functions. If your organization is building toward NIST AI RMF alignment, Clearance accelerates that work materially.

PCI DSS Cardholder Data Environments

AI agents that touch cardholder data environments are in scope for PCI DSS the same way any other system is. Your existing PCI program was not built to account for agent principals. Clearance extends your PCI controls to cover agents operating in or near your cardholder data environment.

Honest Limits

What Clearance does not do.

Security vendors oversell. We are going to be direct with you about what this product does not do, before you waste time on a call.

—

Clearance does not evaluate agent output quality or accuracy. It governs agent behavior at the call and action level. Whether an agent produced a good answer is outside its scope.

—

Clearance does not train, fine-tune, or optimize AI models. If you are looking for a model evaluation or red-teaming tool, this is not it.

—

Clearance does not replace your SOC or incident response team. It adds a governance layer your stack currently lacks. It is not a detection and response product.

—

Clearance does not provide agent runtime infrastructure. It sits alongside your existing infrastructure, not underneath it. You run your agents. Clearance governs them.

—

Clearance will not support every agent framework at launch. We will tell you exactly which frameworks are supported before you commit to anything. If your stack is not on that list, we will tell you clearly and not waste your time.

—

Clearance does not make promises about regulatory outcomes. It produces evidence. Your legal and compliance team determines what that evidence means for your specific obligations.
